About Me

Hello! My name is Devin, and I am a doctoral researcher based at Northwestern Linguistics. Broadly speaking, my academic interests center around applications of computational linguistics to questions in political communication, with emphasis on modern, East Asian politics. In addition, I enjoy learning and practicing to be a better language teacher. I currently work with Rob Voigt on analyzing narratives found on YouTube relating to the political status of Taiwan.

I completed my bachelor's degree in linguistics and computer science at University of Wisconsin - Madison, and I received my master’s degree in computational linguistics from University of Washington. At Washington, I had a great experience at the CLMBR Lab, advised by Shane Steinert-Threlkeld. In addition, I worked at the Center for an Informed Public with Kolina Koltai, studying vaccine/mask-related narratives on Facebook and Instagram during the COVID-19 pandemic. For more detail on my past works, please see my CV.
