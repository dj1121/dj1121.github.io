About Me

Hello! My name is Devin, and I am a researcher in linguistics based at Northwestern University. I completed my undergraduate degree in linguistics and computer science at University of Wisconsin - Madison, and I received my master’s degree in computational linguistics from University of Washington. At Washington, I had a great experience at the CLMBR Lab, advised by Shane Steinert-Threlkeld. In addition, I worked at the Center for an Informed Public with Kolina Koltai, studying vaccine/mask-related narratives on Facebook and Instagram during the COVID-19 pandemic.

These days, my research interests tend to revolve around Mandarin Chinese/Japanese language, narratives in mass/social media, and applications of computational linguistics to social policy. I also enjoy creating translations and learning how to be a better language teacher.
